Moros Intrepudeus

About:

Moros Intrepudeus is a genus of tyrannosauroid theropod that lived during the Late Cretaceous period in what is now Utah, United States. It contains a single species, M. intrepidus. Moros represents one of the earliest known diagnostic Tyrannosauroid material from the North America.

Weight:

Moros Intrepudeus was a small-bodied, cursorial Tyrannosauroid with an estimated weight of about 78 kg (172 lb). The foot bones of Moros were extremely slender, with metatarsal proportions found to be more similar to ornithomimids than to other Late Cretaceous Tyrannosauroids.

Height:

Weighing about 170 pounds and standing less than five feet tall, the newly named species Moros Intrepidus is one of the smallest dinosaurs of its kind dating back to the Cretaceous period, the time between 66 million and 145 million years ago.
